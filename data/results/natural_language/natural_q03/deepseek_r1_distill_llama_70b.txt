Question    : Someone got splashed with a possible toxic substance, maybe in the eyes and on clothing. What should we do first to avoid exposing more crew and clean them up?

TRACK 1: Pure Text Vector Baseline
------------------------------------------------------------
Sources retrieved : 1
Answer:
1. **Rescuer Safety**: Take respiratory, eye, and skin precautions to minimize exposure to more crew before assisting the patient.

2. **Patient Decontamination**:
   - **Skin Exposure**:
     1. Quickly remove soiled clothing.
     2. Brush off visible particulate.
     3. Wipe liquids off the patient with a towel.
     4. Irrigate contaminated areas with water.
   - **Eye Exposure**:
     1. Immediately flush eyes with water from a Drink Bag.
     2. If necessary, configure the eyewash station in the SM at the CBO-3B port and continue irrigation until pain resolves or as directed by the Surgeon.
