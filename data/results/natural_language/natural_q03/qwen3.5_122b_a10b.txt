Answer:
Based on the Toxic Exposure procedure (1.106), here are the first steps to avoid exposing more crew and clean up:

**1. RESCUER SAFETY (First Priority)**
- Take respiratory, eye, and skin precautions prior to removing patient from exposure
- This minimizes the number of crew exposed

**2. DECONTAMINATING PATIENT**

**For Skin/Clothing Exposure:**
- Quickly remove soiled clothes
- Brush off visible particulate and wipe liquids off patient with Towel
- Irrigate contaminated areas with water

**For Eye Exposure:**
- If wearing contact lenses, remove and discard
- Immediately flush with water from Drink Bag
- Configure eyewash station in the SM at the CBO-3B port, then irrigate eye until pain resolves or directed to stop by Surgeon
- If required, refer to 2.3.304 EYE PROCEDURE - EYEWASH KIT USE

**Required Items:**
- Drink Bag
- Towel
