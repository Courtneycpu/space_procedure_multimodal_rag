Question    : Someone got splashed with a possible toxic substance, maybe in the eyes and on clothing. What should we do first to avoid exposing more crew and clean them up?
Timestamp   : 2026-07-06 17:44:33
============================================================

TRACK 1: Pure Text Vector Baseline
------------------------------------------------------------
Sources retrieved : 1
Answer:
1.  **RESCUER SAFETY**: To minimize the number of crew exposed, take respiratory, eye, and skin precautions prior to removing patient from exposure.
2.  **DECONTAMINATING PATIENT**:
    *   **2.1 Skin Exposure**: Quickly remove soiled clothes. Brush off visible particulate and wipe liquids off patient with Towel. Irrigate contaminated areas with water.
    *   **2.2 Eye Exposure**: If wearing contact lenses, remove and discard. Immediately flush with water from Drink Bag. Configure eyewash station in the SM at the CBO-3B port, then irrigate eye until pain resolves or directed to stop by Surgeon.
