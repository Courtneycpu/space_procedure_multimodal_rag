Question    : A crewmate broke a tooth and I can see the sensitive inner part. What pain control and Eugenol steps do we use, and what tissue contact should I avoid?

Answer:
To address the situation where a crewmate has a broken tooth with exposed pulp, follow these steps:

**Pain Control:**
1. Administer two (2) Ibuprofen tablets (400mg each) from the Convenience Medication Pack (White).
2. Administer one (1) Hydrocodone and Acetaminophen tablet (10mg/300mg) from the Oral Medication Pack (Purple).

**Eugenol Application:**
1. Don Nitrile Gloves to handle Eugenol safely.
2. Moisten a small portion of a Gauze 4"x4" with Eugenol from the Topical & Injectable Medication Pack (Brown).
3. Squeeze excess Eugenol onto another Gauze or a Dry Wipe to avoid excess exposure.
4. Place the moistened Gauze over the exposed pulp and have the patient hold it for 1 to 2 minutes.

**Precautions:**
- Minimize contact between Eugenol and skin or gums to prevent tissue damage.
- Avoid chewing on the affected side and keep food out of the cavity.

**Documentation:**
- Use the Digital Camera to photo document the tooth and mouth for further assessment.

This approach ensures proper pain management and careful handling of Eugenol to prevent further tissue damage.
