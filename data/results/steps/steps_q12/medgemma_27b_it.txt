Question    : A crewmate broke a tooth and I can see the sensitive inner part. What pain control and Eugenol steps do we use, and what tissue contact should I avoid?
Timestamp   : 2026-07-06 14:47:16
============================================================

TRACK 1: Pure Text Vector Baseline
------------------------------------------------------------
Sources retrieved : 2
Answer:
1.  **Pain Control:**
    *   Take two (2) tablets Ibuprofen (Motrin) (400mg per tablet) [Convenience Medication Pack (White)].
    *   Take one (1) tablet Hydrocodone and Acetaminophen (Vicodin HP) (10mg/300mg per tablet) [Oral Medication Pack (Purple)].
    *   If required, refer to 2.0.402 TREATING PAIN.
    *   Consult with Surgeon on type of block to place.
    *   Perform 2.6.301 DENTAL PROCEDURE - NERVE BLOCK.

2.  **Treating with Eugenol:**
    *   Don Nitrile Gloves.
    *   Moisten a small portion of a Gauze 4"x4" with Eugenol [Topical & Injectable Medication Pack (Brown)].
    *   Squeeze excess Eugenol out of Gauze 4"x4" onto a second piece of Gauze 4"x4" or a Dry Wipe.
    *   Place moistened portion of Gauze 4"x4" over exposed pulp.
    *   Have patient hold gauze with teeth for 1 to 2 minutes.

3.  **Tissue Contact to Avoid:**
    *   Minimize contact between the Eugenol and skin or gums to prevent tissue damage.

    *   Avoid chewing on affected side.
    *   Keep food out of cavity.
