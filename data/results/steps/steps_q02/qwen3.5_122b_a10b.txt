Based on the retrieved context for AED-Assisted CPR with one rescuer available:

**One Rescuer CPR Procedure:**

1. **1 ASSESS**
   - Assess environment for safety hazards
   - Assess patient status

2. **Deploy AED**
   - Connect AED electrodes to patient's chest
   - Turn AED 'ON' (green) and press
   - Follow verbal prompts (or read prompts on screen if inaudible)

3. **Perform CPR**
   - Perform 30 chest compressions for every 2 breaths (1 second each breath)
   - Continue until prompted by AED
   - Repeat after each 'Start CPR' prompt from AED

4. **Monitor Patient Response**
   - After AED prompts 'Start CPR', perform a 10-second pulse check
   - If pulse detected: Stop chest compressions, assist breathing as necessary
   - If no pulse detected: Continue CPR (go to step 7)

**Note:** This procedure is specifically for AED-assisted CPR. If patient begins spontaneous breathing or pulse is detected, stop chest compressions and assist breathing as necessary.

Answer:
1. **ASSESS** (for one rescuer):
   - Assess environment for safety hazards
   - Assess patient status

2. **If patient unresponsive and not breathing or no normal breathing (only gasping)**:
   - Relocate to and restrain patient on the Crew Medical Restraint System (as required)
   - Page additional crewmember for assistance
   - Call MCC-H to request PMC

3. **PERFORM CPR**:
   - Perform 30 chest compressions for every 2 breaths (1 second each breath) for 5 cycles (approximately 2 minutes)

4. **DEPLOY AED**:
   - Connect AED electrodes to patient's chest
   - Turn AED 'ON' (green) and press
   - Follow verbal prompts
   - If verbal prompts inaudible, read prompts on screen

5. **Continue CPR**:
   - Perform 30 chest compressions for every 2 breaths (1 second each breath) until prompted by AED
   - Repeat after each 'Start CPR' prompt from AED

6. **If patient begins spontaneous breathing or a pulse is detected**:
   - Stop chest compressions
   - Assist breathing as necessary
