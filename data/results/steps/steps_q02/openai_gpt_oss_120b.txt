**CPR with a single rescuer**

1. **Assess** – Check the environment for safety hazards and assess the patient’s status.  
2. **Deploy the AED** – Connect the AED electrodes to the patient’s chest, turn the AED ON, and follow its verbal (or visual) prompts.  
3. **Perform CPR** – When the AED prompts “Start CPR,” give **30 chest compressions** followed by **2 breaths** (each breath ≈ 1 second).  
4. **Repeat** – After each “Start CPR” prompt from the AED, repeat the 30‑compressions/2‑breaths cycle until the AED advises otherwise or a pulse/spontaneous breathing is detected.

**One‑rescuer CPR (AED‑assisted)**  

1. **Assess**  
   - Assess the environment for safety hazards.  
   - Assess the patient’s status.  

2. **Prepare the patient** (as required)  
   - Relocate and restrain the patient on the Crew Medical Restraint System.  

3. **Deploy the AED**  
   - Connect the AED electrodes to the patient’s chest.  
   - Turn the AED “ON” (green) and press the power button.  
   - Follow the AED’s verbal prompts; if the prompts are inaudible, read them on the screen.  

4. **Perform CPR**  
   - Deliver **30 chest compressions** followed by **2 rescue breaths** (each breath ≈ 1 second).  
   - Continue this 30‑compressions/2‑breaths cycle until the AED prompts you to pause or to resume compressions.  
   - After each “Start CPR” prompt from the AED, repeat the cycle.  

5. **Re‑assess**  
   - If the patient begins spontaneous breathing or a pulse is detected, **stop chest compressions** and provide assisted breathing as needed.  

(Repeat steps 3–4 as directed by the AED until advanced care is available.)
