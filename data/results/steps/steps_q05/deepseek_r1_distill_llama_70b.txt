Question    : My crewmate is showing signs of a serious allergic reaction and I have the EpiPen. Where exactly do I inject it, how long do I hold it, and can I give another dose if symptoms come back?

Answer:
To administer the EpiPen to your crewmate experiencing a serious allergic reaction:

1. **Remove the EpiPen** from its protective plastic tubing.
2. **Activate the auto-injector** by pulling off the safety cap with your thumb, forming a fist around the device.
3. **Inject into the outer thigh** using a firm jabbing motion, holding it perpendicular and pressing until a click is heard or felt. Hold for 10 seconds.
4. **Dispose of the used EpiPen** by placing it back in the protective tubing, securing it with Gray Tape, and discarding it. Contact MCC-H to request a PMC.
5. **Repeat if necessary** every 20 minutes if symptoms recur or worsen.

Ensure not to inject through clothing or cover the needle end with your fingers to prevent accidental injury.

To administer the EpiPen to your crewmate showing signs of a serious allergic reaction, follow these steps:

1. **Remove the EpiPen** from its protective plastic tubing.
2. **Form a fist** around the EpiPen with your thumb closest to the safety cap.
3. **Activate the auto-injector** by pulling off the safety cap.
4. **Inject into the outer thigh**: Using a jabbing motion, press the needle end perpendicular to and firmly against the patient's outer thigh until a click is heard or felt.
5. **Hold firmly** against the thigh for **10 seconds**.
6. **Discard the used EpiPen** in its original protective plastic tubing, securing it with Gray Tape.

If symptoms recur or progress, **another dose may be given every 20 minutes** if available.

**Warnings**:
- Do not put fingers over the needle end to prevent self-injection.
- Do not inject through clothing to avoid needle contamination.

After administration, call MCC-H to request further assistance.
