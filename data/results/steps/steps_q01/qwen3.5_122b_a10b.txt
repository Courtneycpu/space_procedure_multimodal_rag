**Emergency Procedure: Severe Allergic Reaction (1.101)**

**Items Required:**
- Gray Tape
- Emergency Medical Treatment Pack (Red): Epinephrine (EpiPen)

**Steps:**

1. Remove Epinephrine (EpiPen) from protective plastic tubing.

2. Form a fist around Epinephrine (EpiPen) with thumb closest to safety cap.

3. Activate auto injector by pulling off safety cap.

4. Using a jabbing motion, press the needle end perpendicular to and firmly against patient's outer thigh until a click is heard or felt. Hold firmly against thigh for 10 seconds.

**Warnings:**
- Do not put fingers over needle end of Epinephrine (EpiPen) to prevent injection into finger.
- Do not give shot through clothing to prevent needle contamination.

**Emergency Procedure: 1.101 SEVERE ALLERGIC REACTION**

**Items Required:**
- Gray Tape
- Emergency Medical Treatment Pack (Red): Epinephrine (EpiPen)

**Steps:**

1. Remove Epinephrine (EpiPen) from protective plastic tubing.

2. Form a fist around Epinephrine (EpiPen) with thumb closest to safety cap.

3. Activate auto injector by pulling off safety cap.

4. Using a jabbing motion, press the needle end perpendicular to and firmly against patient's outer thigh until a click is heard or felt. Hold firmly against thigh for 10 seconds.

5. Discard used Epinephrine (EpiPen) in original protective plastic tubing; secure in tube with Gray Tape and discard. Call MCC-H to request PMC.

6. Epinephrine (EpiPen) may be repeated every 20 minutes if symptoms recur or progress.

7. Go to 2.0.403 ALLERGIC REACTION, all (SODF: MED CL: EXAMS, PROCEDURES, AND TREATMENT).

**Warnings:**
- Do not put fingers over needle end of Epinephrine (EpiPen) to prevent injection into finger.
- Do not give shot through clothing to prevent needle contamination.
