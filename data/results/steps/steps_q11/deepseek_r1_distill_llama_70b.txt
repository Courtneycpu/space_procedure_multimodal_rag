Question    : A crewmate lost a filling and there is a cavity. How do we pack the temporary filling material, and how long should they avoid food or drinking afterward?

Answer:
To pack the temporary filling material and care for the crewmate afterward, follow these steps:

**Packing the Temporary Filling Material:**

1. **Prepare the Material:**
   - Puncture the end of the Temporary Tooth Filling.
   - Squeeze the material onto your finger.
   - Roll the material into a ball.

2. **Place and Shape the Filling:**
   - Place the Temporary Tooth Filling into the tooth cavity and gently form it to the tooth.
   - Wipe off any excess material with a Gauze 4"x4".
   - Have the crewmate firmly bite down on the filling for 2 minutes to allow it to form to the tooth surfaces and harden.
   - Adjust the filling by removing and smoothing excess using Gauze 4"x4" and a Dental Carver File (G).

**Post-Filling Care:**

- The filling will achieve its initial set in 5 minutes.
- The crewmate can resume drinking after approximately 1 hour.
- They should avoid solid or hard food consumption on the affected side of the mouth for the first 12 to 24 hours while the filling hardens.
