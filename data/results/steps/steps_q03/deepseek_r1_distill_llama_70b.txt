Question    : What medication is used to treat a seizure and how is it administered?

Answer:
The medications used to treat a seizure are Diazepam (Valium) and Phenytoin (Dilantin ER). 

1. **Diazepam (Valium)** is administered as an intramuscular injection. The dosage is 2 mL at 5 mg/mL, totaling 10 mg. If supplied in a prefilled syringe, follow procedure 2.14.302. If in a vial, follow procedure 2.14.303. 

2. **Phenytoin (Dilantin ER)** is given orally. One capsule is administered when the patient is awake and able to swallow. A second dose is given two hours later or as directed by the surgeon.

If the seizure continues for more than 5 minutes or recurs, the medication administration is repeated after 10 minutes.
