1.  **PROTECTING FROM INJURY**
    *   Protect patient from injury by preventing impact of the body with hard structures.
    *   Attempt to locate the patient in the center of a module away from structures.
    *   Never attempt to pry open or put anything in the mouth during a seizure.
    *   Seizures typically last less than a minute.

2.  **ADMINISTERING INTRAMUSCULAR SHOT**
    *   **2.1 Configure Medication for Injection**
        *   Prepare 2 mL Diazepam (Valium) 5 mg/mL for a total of 10 mg [Topical & Injectable Medication Pack (Brown)].
        *   If Diazepam (Valium) supplied in a prefilled syringe, perform 2.14.302 INJECTIONS AND IV – MEDICINE PREPARATION FOR INJECTION, all (SODF: MED CL: EXAMS, PROCEDURES, AND TREATMENT), then: Go to step 2.2.
        *   If Diazepam (Valium) supplied in a vial, perform 2.14.303 INJECTIONS AND IV - DRAWING UP MEDICINE FROM VIAL, all (SODF: MED CL: EXAMS, PROCEDURES, AND TREATMENT), then: Go to step 2.2.
    *   **2.2 Administer Injection of Medication**
        *   Give 2 mL intramuscular shot of Diazepam (Valium) 5 mg/mL.
        *   If required, refer to 2.14.304 INJECTIONS AND IV - INTRAMUSCULAR SHOT, all (SODF: MED CL: EXAMS, PROCEDURES, AND TREATMENT).
    *   If seizure continues for > 5 minutes or for repeated seizures, repeat medication administration in 10 minutes.

3.  **ADMINISTERING MEDICATION**
    *   **7.2** When awake, coherent, and able to swallow, give one capsule of Phenytoin (Dilantin ER) by mouth, as directed by Surgeon [Oral Medication Pack (Purple)].
    *   **7.3** After two hours, give a second dose of Phenytoin (Dilantin ER) by mouth, or as directed by Surgeon.

Medications used:
*   Diazepam (Valium) 5 mg/mL: Administered as a 2 mL intramuscular shot (10 mg total).
*   Phenytoin (Dilantin ER): Administered orally (one capsule, then a second dose after two hours, or as directed by Surgeon).
