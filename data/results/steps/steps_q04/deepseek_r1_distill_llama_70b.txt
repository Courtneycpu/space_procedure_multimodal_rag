Question    : How do you help someone who is choking and no longer conscious?

Answer:
To help someone who is choking and no longer conscious, follow these steps:

1. **Perform Abdominal Thrusts**:
   - Position behind the patient.
   - Make a fist and place it above the belly button but below the ribcage.
   - Pull sharply inward and upward.
   - Repeat until the object clears or the patient becomes unconscious.

2. **Clear Airway and Give Breaths**:
   - Open the airway using the Head tilt-Chin lift method.
   - Inspect for a foreign object and remove if easily accessible.
   - Pinch the nose and attempt to give breaths.

3. **Proceed to AED-Assisted CPR**:
   - If the patient is unresponsive and not breathing normally, go to procedure 1.102 AED ASSISTED CPR.

4. **Obtain Vital Signs**:
   - Use the Medical Diagnostic Pack to take pulse, respiratory rate, and blood pressure.
   - Record the data for later reporting to the Surgeon.

5. **Consult the Surgeon**:
   - Call MCC-H to request PMC (Procedure Monitoring Console) assistance.

This procedure ensures a structured approach to handling a choking emergency when the patient loses consciousness.
