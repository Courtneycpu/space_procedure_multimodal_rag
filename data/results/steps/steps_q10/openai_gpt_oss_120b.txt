Question    : For difficulty breathing, how do I count pulse and respiratory rate, administer oxygen, and give inhaled Albuterol?

TRACK 1: Pure Text Vector Baseline
------------------------------------------------------------
Sources retrieved : 1
Answer:
**1. Count pulse and respiratory rate**  
1. Measure the pulse for **15 seconds** and multiply the count by 4.  
2. Measure the respiratory rate for **15 seconds** and multiply the count by 4.  
3. Record both values for later reporting to the Surgeon.  

**2. Administer oxygen**  
1. Retrieve the **Respiratory Support Pack**.  
2. Locate the **Cue Card** in the pack’s lid pocket.  
3. Provide oxygen using a **Low‑Flow Non‑Rebreather Mask** as shown on the cue card.  

**3. Give inhaled Albuterol (Proventil HFA)**  
1. Shake the Albuterol inhaler to mix the medication.  
2. Remove the cap (inhaler is stored in the **Topical & Injectable Medication Pack (Brown)**).  
3. Have the patient **fully exhale** and place the inhaler in the mouth.  
4. Instruct the patient to **inhale slowly** through the mouth and **activate** the inhaler.  
5. Have the patient **hold the breath for 8–10 seconds**, then exhale.  
6. **Deliver a second dose** following the same procedure.
