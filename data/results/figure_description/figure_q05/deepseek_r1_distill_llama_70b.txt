To prepare a dental nerve block, configure the syringe for one-handed aspiration, and inject Lidocaine for upper or lower nerve block, follow these steps:

### Preparation:
1. Draw up 10 mL of Lidocaine (Xylocaine) into a 10 mL syringe. Refer to document 2.14.303 for detailed instructions on drawing medicine from a vial.

### Configuring the Syringe for One-Handed Aspiration:
1. Tear off an 8-inch strip of Medical Tape from the Medical Supply Pack (Green).
2. Twist the middle of the tape around itself.
3. Remove the plunger from the syringe.
4. Apply the ends of the tape to the plunger.
5. Reassemble the syringe with the modified plunger to allow one-handed aspiration.

### Upper Nerve Block Injection:
1. Insert the needle, directing it toward the root of the tooth to be anesthetized.
2. Gently pull back on the syringe plunger to aspirate and ensure no blood is present in the chamber.
3. Inject 2 to 3 mL of Lidocaine directly over the root of the tooth.
4. Withdraw the needle and recap it using a one-handed technique.
5. Temporarily stow the syringe.

### Lower Nerve Block Injection:
1. Insert the needle just anterior at the point where the seam between the cheek and throat turns upward toward the maxilla.
2. Keep the barrel of the syringe over teeth 3 and 4 on the opposite side of the injection.
3. Insert the needle until bone is contacted, ensuring only 5 to 10 mm of the needle is exposed.
   - If no bone contact is made, withdraw the needle slightly and reposition it with the syringe barrel over teeth 4 and 5.
   - If more than 5 to 10 mm of the needle is exposed, reposition the needle with the syringe barrel over teeth 2 and 3.
4. Gently pull back on the syringe plunger to aspirate and ensure no blood is present in the chamber.
5. Inject 2 to 3 mL of Lidocaine directly over the root of the tooth.
6. Withdraw the needle and recap it using a one-handed technique.
7. Wait 5 minutes. If pain does not subside, repeat the injection.

### Notes:
- Ensure proper aspiration to avoid injecting into a blood vessel.
- Use one-handed technique for recapping the needle.
- Wait 5 minutes after injection to assess pain relief before repeating if necessary.
