**1. CONFIGURING SYRINGE FOR ONE-HANDED ASPIRATION**

1. Tear off an 8-inch strip of Medical Tape [Medical Supply Pack (Green)].
2. Twist the middle of the tape around itself.
3. Pull plunger out of syringe.
4. Apply the ends of the tape to the plunger.

*Note: A standard Syringe 10 mL is modified to allow for one-handed aspiration. Aspiration allows operator to make sure the needle is not in a blood vessel.*

**2. PREPARING MEDICATION**

1. Draw up 10 mL of Lidocaine (Xylocaine) into Syringe 10 mL.

**3. UPPER NERVE BLOCK INJECTION**

1. Insert needle, directing needle toward root of tooth to be anesthetized.
2. Gently pull back on syringe plunger and inspect chamber to ensure no blood in chamber.
3. Inject 2 to 3 mL of Lidocaine (Xylocaine) directly over the root of the tooth.
4. Withdraw needle; recap it using one-handed technique and temporarily stow.

**4. LOWER NERVE BLOCK INJECTION**

1. Insert needle just anterior at point where seam between cheek and throat turns upward toward maxilla.
2. Keep barrel of syringe over teeth 3 and 4 on opposite side of injection.
3. Insert needle until bone is contacted (and only 5 to 10 mm of needle is left exposed).
4. If no bone contact: Withdraw needle slightly, reposition needle with syringe barrel over teeth 4 and 5.
5. If bone is contacted and more than 5 to 10 mm of needle exposed: Reposition needle with syringe barrel over teeth 2 and 3.
6. Gently pull back on syringe plunger and inspect chamber to ensure no blood in chamber.
7. Inject 2 to 3 mL of Lidocaine (Xylocaine) directly over the root of the tooth.
8. Withdraw needle; recap it using one-handed technique and temporarily stow.
9. Wait 5 minutes. Pain should subside.
10. If pain does not subside: Repeat step 5.2.
