Question    : How do I prepare a dental nerve block, configure the syringe for one-handed aspiration, and inject Lidocaine for upper or lower nerve block?

TRACK 1: Pure Text Vector Baseline
------------------------------------------------------------
Sources retrieved : 1
Answer:
1.  **Draw up Lidocaine:** Draw up 10 mL of Lidocaine (Xylocaine) into a Syringe 10 mL. Refer to section 2.14.303 for instructions if needed.
2.  **Configure Syringe for Aspiration:**
    *   Tear off an 8-inch strip of Medical Tape [Medical Supply Pack (Green)].
    *   Twist the middle of the tape around itself.
    *   Pull the plunger out of the syringe.
    *   Apply the ends of the tape to the plunger. (See Figure 1)
3.  **Upper Nerve Block Injection:**
    *   Insert the needle, directing it toward the root of the tooth to be anesthetized.
    *   Gently pull back on the syringe plunger and inspect the chamber to ensure no blood is present.
    *   Inject 2 to 3 mL of Lidocaine (Xylocaine) directly over the root of the tooth.
    *   Withdraw the needle, recap it using one-handed technique, and temporarily stow.
    *   Wait 5 minutes. If pain does not subside, repeat the injection step.
4.  **Lower Nerve Block Injection:**
    *   Insert the needle just anterior at the point where the seam between the cheek and throat turns upward toward the maxilla. Keep the barrel of the syringe over teeth 3 and 4 on the opposite side of the injection. (See Figure 3)
    *   Insert the needle until bone is contacted, leaving only 5 to 10 mm of the needle exposed.
        *   If no bone contact is made, withdraw the needle slightly and reposition it with the syringe barrel over teeth 4 and 5.
        *   If bone is contacted but more than 5 to 10 mm of the needle is exposed, reposition the needle with the syringe barrel over teeth 2 and 3.
    *   Gently pull back on the syringe plunger and inspect the chamber to ensure no blood is present.
    *   Inject 2 to 3 mL of Lidocaine (Xylocaine) directly over the root of the tooth.
    *   Withdraw the needle, recap it using one-handed technique, and temporarily stow.
    *   Wait 5 minutes. If pain does not subside, repeat the injection step.

Answer:
1.  **Prepare the Workspace:** Create a workstation using Gray Tape and temporarily stow hardware on the tape. Use a Ziplock Bag for trash stowage.
2.  **Apply Topical Anesthetic:** Place a Benzocaine Swabstick on the injection site. Leave it in place for 5 to 10 minutes.
3.  **Configure Syringe for One-Handed Aspiration:**
    *   Tear off an 8-inch strip of Medical Tape.
    *   Twist the middle of the tape around itself.
    *   Pull the plunger out of the Syringe 10 mL.
    *   Apply the ends of the tape to the plunger.
4.  **Draw up Lidocaine:** Draw up 10 mL of Lidocaine (Xylocaine) into Syringe 10 mL.
5.  **Prepare the Patient:** Don Nitrile Gloves and LED Headlamp. Pack Gauze 4"x4" between the gum and teeth to assist in visualizing the injection location.
6.  **Select Injection Location:**
    *   If using an upper nerve block, go to step 5.1.
    *   If using a lower nerve block, go to step 5.2.
7.  **Upper Injection Technique (5.1):**
    *   Needle 25G →|← Syringe 10 mL
    *   Remove needle cap and temporarily stow.
    *   Place needle at the height of the moveable membrane (muccobuccal fold) above fixed gum tissue.
    *   Insert needle, directing it toward the root of the tooth to be anesthetized.
    *   Gently pull back on the syringe plunger and inspect the chamber to ensure no blood is present.
    *   Inject 2 to 3 mL of Lidocaine (Xylocaine) directly over the root of the tooth.
    *   Withdraw the needle, recap it using a one-handed technique, and temporarily stow.
    *   Wait 5 minutes. If pain does not subside, repeat step 5.1.
8.  **Lower Injection Technique (5.2):**
    *   Needle 25G →|← Syringe 10 mL
    *   Remove needle cap and temporarily stow.
    *   Place thumb in the deepest portion of the coronoid notch.
    *   Insert needle just anterior at the point where the seam between the cheek and throat turns upward toward the maxilla.
    *   Ensure the barrel of the syringe is kept over teeth 3 and 4 on the opposite side of the injection.
    *   Gently pull back on the syringe plunger and inspect the chamber to ensure no blood is present.
    *   Inject 2 to 3 mL of Lidocaine (Xylocaine) directly over the root of the tooth.
    *   Withdraw the needle, recap it using a one-handed technique, and temporarily stow.
    *   Wait 5 minutes. If pain does not subside, repeat step 5.2.
9.  **Post-Procedure:** Place used hardware, contaminated gloves, and packaging materials in a Ziplock Bag and discard.
