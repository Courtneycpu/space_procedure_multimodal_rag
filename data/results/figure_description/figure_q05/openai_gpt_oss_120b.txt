**1. Prepare the syringe for one‑handed aspiration**

1.1 Tear off an 8‑inch strip of **Medical Tape** (Medical Supply Pack – Green).  
1.2 Twist the middle of the tape around itself to form a loop.  
1.3 Pull the plunger out of a standard **Syringe 10 mL**.  
1.4 Apply the two ends of the tape to the plunger so the tape holds the plunger in place (Figure 1).  
1.5 Draw up **10 mL of Lidocaine (Xylocaine)** into the taped syringe (step 3.2).

**2. Upper nerve block (maxillary)**  

2.1 Insert the needle, directing it toward the root of the tooth to be anesthetized.  
2.2 Gently pull back on the syringe plunger and inspect the chamber; **no blood must be present** (one‑handed aspiration).  
2.3 Inject **2 to 3 mL of Lidocaine (Xylocaine)** directly over the root of the tooth.  
2.4 Withdraw the needle, recap it using the one‑handed technique, and temporarily stow the syringe.

**3. Lower nerve block (mandibular)**  

3.1 Insert the needle just anterior to the point where the seam between cheek and throat turns upward toward the maxilla.  
 • Keep the barrel of the syringe over teeth 3 and 4 on the opposite side of the injection (as noted).  
3.2 Advance the needle until bone is contacted, leaving **5 to 10 mm** of needle exposed.  
 – If bone is not contacted, withdraw slightly and reposition the needle with the barrel over teeth 4 and 5.  
 – If bone is contacted and more than 5–10 mm of needle remains exposed, reposition the barrel over teeth 2 and 3.  
3.3 Gently pull back on the plunger and inspect the chamber; **no blood must be present** (one‑handed aspiration).  
3.4 Inject **2 to 3 mL of Lidocaine (Xylocaine)** directly over the root of the tooth.  
3.5 Withdraw the needle, recap it using the one‑handed technique, and temporarily stow the syringe.  
3.6 Wait **5 minutes**; pain should subside.  
 – If pain does not subside, repeat step 3.4 (inject another 2–3 mL).
