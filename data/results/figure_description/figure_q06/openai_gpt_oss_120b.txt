**After a lost dental crown**

1. **Photo‑document the loss** – Use a Digital Camera to photograph the tooth and the broken fragment.  
2. **Assess pain or sensitivity**  
   * **No pain/sensitivity:** place the crown in a Ziplock Bag and store it in a known location in the sleep station. Proceed to the replacement steps.  
   * **Pain/sensitivity present:** go to the “PREPARING EQUIPMENT” section (step 2) before proceeding.  

**Replacing the crown with Dental Adhesive**

1. **Don nitrile gloves.**  
2. **Prepare the loose crown** (section 3.1)  
   * Remove residual cement from the crown and tooth with the Dental Carver File (G).  
   * Check the fit by placing the crown on the tooth and having the subject bite down.  
   * Remove the crown and dry it with a 4″ × 4″ gauze; temporarily stow the crown in a 4″ × 4″ gauze.  
3. **Prepare Dental Adhesive** (section 2.1)  
   * Remove Dental Adhesive from its packaging and take off the cap.  
   * Press the plunger to dispense a small amount of material onto a 4″ × 4″ gauze, ensuring flow from both chambers of the syringe.  
   * Wipe any residual material from the syringe.  
   * Align the v‑shaped notches on the Dental Adhesive syringe with the Dental Adhesive Tip.  
4. **Apply adhesive and seat the crown** (section 3.2)  
   * Coat the surfaces of the crown that will contact the tooth with the Dental Adhesive.  
   * Place the crown over the tooth, confirming proper orientation.  
   * Place a cotton swab over the crown and have the subject gently bite down on the swab and crown for **3–5 seconds** to fully seat the crown.  
   * Remove the cotton swab and have the subject bite down a second time to check if the crown is fully seated.  
5. **Confirm crown placement** (section 3.3)  
   * **If the crown is fully seated:** place the cotton swab back over the crown and have the subject bite down, applying moderate pressure for **2–3 minutes**.  
   * **If the crown is not fully seated:** carefully remove the crown by prying up at the margins with the Dental Crown Remover (D), then repeat steps 3.1 through 3.3.
