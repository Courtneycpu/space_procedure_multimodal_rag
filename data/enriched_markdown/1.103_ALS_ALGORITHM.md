4 ACTIVATE INTRAOSSEOUS DEVICE
1. Remove safety latch and stow for later use.
2. Maintain positive pressure of blue barrel perpendicular to skin at insertion point. (Figure 3)
3. Place palm of other hand over the gray end of Intraosseous Device and apply gentle pressure to activate. A click will sound as the device activates. (Figure 4)
4. Gently lift Intraosseous Device, exposing the needle unit. 
-> 5

Figure 4.- Activating Intraosseous Device.


5 POST INSERTION STEPS
1. Place safety latch between skin and hub of Intraosseous needle. (Figure 5)
2. Place Medical Tape over safety latch to secure. (Figure 6)
3. Stabilize Intraosseous needle with hand; with other hand, remove inner needle.
4. Remove sterile cover from IV Cap, then attach Syringe and IV Cap assembly to Intraosseous needle. (Figure 6)
5. Pull back on plunger of Syringe >= 5 mL, then disconnect Syringe from blue IV Cap. Blood or fluid return may or may not be seen. This action will potentially clear bone fragments from the needle. (Figure 7) 
-> 6

12 CPR TERMINATION
1. Coordinate with Surgeon, if available.
2. Consider CPR termination if all of the following conditions apply:
At least 10 minutes of CPR since last dose of medication, and
No pulse or spontaneous breathing, and
At least 45 minutes elapsed since start of resuscitation.

RESPIRATORY ASSESSMENT

If AED advises 'PUSH SHOCK BUTTON', or pulse is not detected, follow AED prompts and go to block 1, page 1, Intraosseous Device Insertion.

13 CHECK PATIENT RESPONSE
Is patient awake and able to speak?
-> Yes -> 15
-> No -> 14

14 ASSESS BREATHING
Are any of the following true?
Skin of lips, face, or hands blue/gray.
Breathing rate < 12 or > 30 breaths per minute.
Patient snoring or having difficulty inhaling.
-> No to all -> 15
-> Yes to any -> 16

-> 15 PROVIDE OXYGEN WITH MASK
1. Unstow Respiratory Support Pack
2. Locate Cue Card in lid pocket.
3. Provide oxygen by performing 3.201 RESPIRATORY SUPPORT PACK CUE CARD, Oxygen Via Low Flow Non Rebreather Mask (SODF: MED CL: REFERENCE), then:
-> 17

16 ASSIST BREATHING AND PROVIDE OXYGEN USING AMBU BAG
1. Assist breathing using mouth to mouth breaths or AMBU Bag. (Administer breaths at a rate of one breath every 5 seconds).
2. Unstow Respiratory Support Pack
3. Locate Cue Card in lid pocket.
4. Provide oxygen using AMBU Bag by performing 3.201 RESPIRATORY SUPPORT PACK - CUE CARD, Oxygen Via AMBU Bag (SODF: MED CL: REFERENCE), then:
-> 17
