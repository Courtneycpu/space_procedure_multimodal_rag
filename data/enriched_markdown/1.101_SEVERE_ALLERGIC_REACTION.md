(MED CL/HTV5 - ALL/FIN 2/Paper on ISS/T)

OBJECTIVE:
To rapidly treat a severe allergic reaction.

ITEMS:
Gray Tape
Emergency Medical Treatment Pack (Red):
Epinephrine (EpiPen)

1. DEPLOYING AND USING EPINEPHRINE (EPIPEN)
1.1 Remove Epinephrine (EpiPen) from protective plastic tubing.
(Figure 1)

[Figure 1]

> **[Figure description]** Figure 1 shows an Epinephrine (EpiPen) and its protective plastic tubing. The image contains two main objects: a clear plastic tube with a yellow cap at one end, labeled "Protective Plastic Tubing," and the auto-injector device itself. The auto-injector is a white cylindrical device with a blue "Safety Cap" at one end and an orange "Needle End" at the opposite end. The image serves to identify the components of the Epinephrine (EpiPen) and its storage tubing as part of the deployment and use procedure for treating a severe allergic reaction.


Figure 1.- Epinephrine (EpiPen).

1.2 Form a fist around Epinephrine (EpiPen) with thumb closest to safety cap.

1.3 Activate auto injector by pulling off safety cap.
(Figure 2)

WARNING
1. Do not put fingers over needle end of Epinephrine (EpiPen) to
prevent injection into finger.
2. Do not give shot through clothing to prevent needle contamination.

> **[Figure description]** A person's hands are shown preparing an Epinephrine (EpiPen) auto-injector. The left hand is closed in a fist around the body of the device, while the right hand's thumb and index finger are pulling a blue safety cap away from the top of the injector. 1.4 Using a jabbing motion, press the needle end perpendicular to and firmly
against patient's outer thigh until a click is heard or felt.
Hold firmly against thigh for 10 seconds.
(Figure 3)

[Figure 3]

> **[Figure description]** An illustration showing the correct positioning of an Epinephrine (EpiPen) auto-injector during administration. A person's hand is shown gripping the device in a fist and pressing the needle end perpendicular to and firmly against the outer thigh of a patient. The patient is seated on a wooden surface and wearing blue clothing. This image is labeled as Figure 3 and serves to demonstrate the physical application of the medication to the thigh as described in step 1.4 of the procedure.


Figure 3.- Positioning of Epinephrine (EpiPen).

1.5 Discard used Epinephrine (EpiPen) in original protective plastic tubing;
secure in tube with Gray Tape and discard.
Call MCC-H to request PMC.

1.6 Epinephrine (EpiPen) may be repeated every 20 minutes if symptoms recur
or progress.
